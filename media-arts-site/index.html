Biography

My name is Haruzumi Furukawa, and I am deeply interested in the relationship between art and science. After seeing Salvador Dali's melting clock painting, I became curious about how visual art can express ideas about time and space. That image made me think about Einstein's theory of relativity and how perception can change depending on where we stand. In my Media Arts work, I want to explore imagination, multiple dimensions, and the way time can feel fluid instead of fixed. I am especially interested in creating images that combine emotion with scientific concepts. Through this class, I hope to develop an intuitive artistic sense that connects thought, feeling, and visual form.

Time, Space, and Perception

XII III VI IX

"Where time softens, space begins to move."

My Artwork

Artwork title placeholder

Through magnification and metamorphosis, this piece reveals hidden distortions within space. From a distance it appears stable and familiar, yet up close the structure of space begins to bend. The work reflects the idea that space and perception are not absolute, echoing concepts found in Einstein’s theory of relativity.

This piece explores a moment where time appears to stretch and soften. I used layered forms and drifting edges to suggest that reality can bend with perception. The blurred atmosphere is inspired by the feeling of melting time, where memory, motion, and space overlap into one visual field.
